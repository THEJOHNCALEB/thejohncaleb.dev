There's a saying I think about a lot. A moving man will always meet his luck. A stationary man stays where he is and waits for luck to find him. It never does.

I didn't fully understand this until I looked back at the last two years of my life and realized almost every good thing that happened came from movement. Not from careful planning. Not from being ready. Just from doing something.

## luck is a numbers game

Nobody got lucky sitting in their room. Every opportunity I've had came from putting myself in situations where opportunities could happen. Going to events. Reaching out to people. Building things and putting them online. Applying for things I wasn't fully qualified for.

Most of those attempts went nowhere. But one in ten didn't. And that one in ten was enough.

The guy who offered me my first freelance gig found me because I had posted a project on whatsapp. I wasn't posting for clients. I was just sharing what I was building and he reached out. That's not luck. That's being visible enough for luck to find you.

## don't wait until you're ready

I almost didn't apply for the job I have now because I thought I wasn't qualified. Someone convinced me to apply anyway. I got the interview. I got the job.

The version of me that waited until he felt ready would still be waiting. The version of me that applied anyway is working on real products with real users.

This applies to everything. Don't wait to start posting until you have something impressive to share. Don't wait to apply until you meet every requirement. Don't wait to build until you've learned every prerequisite. Movement creates readiness, not the other way around.

## small moves count

Movement doesn't have to be dramatic. You don't need to quit school or move to a new city or launch a startup. Small moves compound.

Pushing a commit every day. Sending one cold email a week. Writing one blog post a month. Posting one thing you built. These feel insignificant in the moment but over months and years they add up to something real.

The people who seem lucky are usually just people who've been making small moves consistently for longer than you've been paying attention.

## the flip side

The opposite is also true. Staying still is comfortable but it's also the fastest way to go nowhere. I've had periods where I stopped building, stopped posting, stopped reaching out. Nothing bad happened. But nothing good happened either. The world just kept moving while I stayed in place.

Those periods taught me that momentum isn't something you have, it's something you maintain. You can't store it up. If you stop moving, it's gone. And rebuilding it is harder than keeping it going.

So keep moving. Build things. Post them. Talk to people. Apply for stuff. Most of it won't work. But some of it will. And that's the whole point.
